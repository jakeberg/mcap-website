DR. EMERSON R. HALL, JR.


Dr. Emerson ‘Randy’ Hall, Jr., has a long professional career in strategic marketing and program effectiveness, including activating patient advocates, professional societies, thought leaders, consumers and other stakeholders to meet both corporate and not-for-profit objectives. He is highly skilled at building coalitions and creating programs to improve health care awareness and treatment, mentoring and skills development, and economic and business development.

Randy has led efforts to drive tens of millions of patients to better health outcomes. He has also been responsible for generating hundreds of millions of dollars in revenues within the pharmaceutical, information technology and market research industries. He has an impeccable record of helping and mentoring hundreds of persons as well as supporting mentoring organizations such as Media Career Advancement Program, Mentoring in Medicine, 100 Black Men of America, and others.

Randy has decades of helping not-for profit organizations, and has been on the board and/or volunteered for numerous groups including the National Medical Association, National Osteoporosis Foundation, Foundation for Sarcoidosis Research, St. Vincent’s Hospital Network, Habitat for Humanity, Save The Children, New Federal Theater, The Johns Hopkins University, Howard University Public Television, the Coppin State University Development Foundation, and many more.

Randy is a recipient of many honors, including the Dedicated Advocate for the Underserved Award from the Association of Clinicians for the Underserved; the Award for Outstanding Service from the American Diabetes Association; a Telly Award for the mental health stigma-busting PSA series, Beating the Blue;, Dedication to Campaign on Clinical Depression from The National Mental Health Association; and, the Life Time Achievement Award from Coppin State University.

Randy has a Ph.D. in Public Service from the University of Maryland System and a Bachelor of Science degree from The John Hopkins University. He has also attended and conducted course work at George Washington University’s Masters in Telecommunications Management, Georgetown University Graduate Studies in Legislative Affairs, and the University of Pennsylvania Law School.

In his spare time, Randy, Gentle Grey Wolf, (tribal name) helps his Nanticoke Indian Association and people. He facilitates entrepreneurial efforts, and travels extensively, enjoying the world’s best deep sea fishing and great vacations with his loving wife, Dr. Freda Lewis-Hall, and family members.
