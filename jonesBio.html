DR. NATHANIEL JONES


Superintendent Nathaniel (Nate) Jones is a native of Indianapolis, IN. He received his formal education from the Indianapolis Public School system (IPS). In pursuit of a college degree, his passion for educating students evolved. Upon completion of the degree program, he decided to give back to the community by teaching in the Indianapolis Public School system; he taught for 5 years in elementary and adult education. In 1980, he left IPS to begin his administrative career in the Metropolitan School District of Washington Township in Indianapolis, Indiana; he was one of the youngest principals (27 years of age) in the state of Indiana. In the Washington Township School system he served over 25 years in numerous positions such as, Principal, Director of Elementary Education and Assistant Superintendent of Curriculum/Instruction. All schools where Mr. Jones served as principal reached state and national recognition for academic achievement and exceptional leadership.

During his career, he received three degrees, certifications, two (2) “Distinguished Alumni Awards” from Indiana University, Bloomington, IN., the prestigious Milken Educator Award (a $25,000.00 award), the Center for Leadership (CLD) Achiever Award, State of Indiana Sagamore of the Wabash Recipient, Congressional Salute, and he was selected as one of the 100 top administrators in North America by the Executive Educator. Likewise, he has received over 40 local, state, and national awards for his leadership and innovative approach to educating students, teachers, and the community. Superintendent Jones has accepted numerous invitations to conduct and/or participate in conferences throughout the United States; his input is greatly valued. In addition, he is a lifetime member of the NAACP. In 2011, he was featured in the Education Executive Publication as a Trendsetter, and Who’s Who in Black Indianapolis. Superintendent Jones has served on numerous not-for-profit Organization Boards. He has received letters of appreciation and proclamations for his service to education from President Obama, Governor Pence, Senator Greg Taylor, Congressman Andre Carson, and State Representative Cherrish Pryor. In 2003, he became the first African American Superintendent to accept the awesome responsibility to lead the Metropolitan School District of Pike Township in Indianapolis, IN. In addition, he is the first African American Superintendent who graduated from the Indianapolis Public School system and serves in this role in Indiana. During his tenure as Superintendent, graduation rate has been 92 – 94%; high school recognized as one of the most challenging in the nation, increased scholarships/grants, highest number of Gates Scholars in Indiana, Implemented STEM Center, recipient of 2 District of Distinction Awards, spearheaded major construction/renovation (all facilities are ready for 21st Century), earned over $75 million for Federal Competitive Grants (2005-15) in Awards. Superintendent Jones has over 43 years of experience in the field of education.

He is not only committed to educating students and serving the community, he is also dedicated to his family, Cynthia Jones (wife), two married children, four grandsons, a daughter-in-law and a son-in-law.
