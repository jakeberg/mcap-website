Nate Pass


    Nate is an active member of the Indianapolis Chapter of the 100 Black Men of America mentoring programs and is a recipient of the 2009 Indiana Minority Business Champion of Diversity Award for his work in developing youth for successful careers in broadcasting. Mr. Pass has won Emmy awards and the prestigious 2009 Broadcast Engineering Excellence Awards for his designs and building state-of-the-art broadcast media studios and production facilities.

    Broadcasting executives consistently praised Nate for his leadership in mentoring. His commitment to education and teaching is highlighted by his early development of a TV Internship program providing education and jobs for the community’s youth.

    He has also distinguished himself as a superb role model. Working in his profession over two decades, his competence, compassion and ethics are exemplary. In providing a broad spectrum of assessment he has more than proven his ability to lead small and large complex engineering projects. He regularly speaks on these topics with other broadcast industry professionals.

    Nate’s grasp of both leadership and technical skills comes "packaged" in a wonderfully friendly, witty, sincere and caring human being. His personality is delightful. He is sensitive, flexible, respected and admired by peers, subordinates and superiors alike. He shines with humility.

    Nate has a Degree in Engineering and Communications, multiple technical certifications, and memberships in the National Association of Broadcasters and the Society of Broadcast Engineers,

    Nate has substantial experience in teaming with senior management and executive directors to develop strategic plans for new business ventures and to build media facilities. He has demonstrated outstanding talents in integration and managerial skills as well as in practice. He has a reputation as a highly respected professional and author of his work in broadcasting worldwide publications.

    He was formerly employed by two major TV networks (ABC, NBC) and contributed to numerous TV shows including ABC Wide World of Sports, Olympics, Football, Baseball, NBC News, Today Show, David Letterman and Saturday Night Live.
